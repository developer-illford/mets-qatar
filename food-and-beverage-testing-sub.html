Food & Beverage Analysis in Qatar

Ensuring Food Safety and Quality from Farm to Fork

In Qatar’s rapidly expanding food and beverage sector, product safety, nutritional integrity, and regulatory compliance are vital to consumer trust. With rising imports, growing local production, and global supply chains, every stage — from raw material sourcing to final packaging — demands rigorous food testing and quality assurance.

Our Qatar-based ISO/IEC 17025 accredited laboratory provides a complete range of food and beverage testing services in full compliance with Qatari and international standards (GSO, QCS, ISO). We ensure your food and beverage products meet the safety, labeling, and hygiene benchmarks set by Qatar’s Ministry of Public Health (MoPH) and global regulatory bodies.

Qatar Food Regulations, Standards & Guidelines

Food and beverage testing in Qatar is governed by stringent national and regional frameworks designed to protect consumers and support fair trade.

Our methods strictly adhere to:

  • Qatar General Organization for Standards and Metrology (QS/QCS)

  • GCC Standardization Organization (GSO) standards

  • Qatar Food Law No. 8 of 1990 and its amendments

  • Qatar Ministry of Public Health (MoPH) – Food Safety Department Regulations

  • Codex Alimentarius (as referenced in Qatari imports and trade standards)

  • Codex Alimentariusguidelines (as referenced in Qatari import and export policies)

Our testing procedures are accredited under ISO/IEC 17025, ensuring analytical precision, traceability, and international recognition of results under Qatar Conformity Assessment (QGOSM).

Comprehensive Food & Beverage Testing Services

Our laboratory offers end-to-end testing solutions covering microbiological, chemical, physical, and sensory parameters for foods and beverages.

1. Microbiological Analysis

Tests Include:

  • Total Plate Count
  • Yeasts and Molds
  • Coliforms / E. coli
  • Salmonella spp.
  • Listeria monocytogenes
  • Staphylococcus aureus

Purpose :

To detect microbial contamination, assess hygiene standards, and validate product shelf-life compliance.



2. Chemical and Nutritional Analysis

Parameters Tested:

  • Proximate Composition: Protein, Fat, Moisture, Ash, Fiber, Carbohydrates
  • Nutritional Label Verification (per GSO 9/2013)
  • pH and Acidity Analysis
  • Quantification of Preservatives, Additives, and Sweeteners
  • Heavy Metals (Lead, Mercury, Cadmium, Arsenic)

Purpose :

To confirm nutritional labeling accuracy, ingredient safety, and compliance with MoPH food regulations.



3. Physical and Sensory Analysis

Tests Include:

  • Texture, Color, and Appearance
  • Sensory Evaluation Panels
  • Shelf-Life and Stability Studies

Purpose :

To evaluate product consistency, consumer acceptability, and quality retention over time.



4. Water and Beverage Testing

Parameters Covered:

  • pH, Conductivity, and Total Dissolved Solids (TDS)
  • Microbial Quality (HPC, Coliforms, Pseudomonas)
  • Mineral and Trace Element Profiling
  • Compliance with GSO 1025/2009 (Bottled Drinking Water Standards)

Purpose :

To ensure that bottled water, juices, carbonated drinks, and other beverages comply with Qatar’s safety and purity regulations.
