Environmental Monitoring Services

Ensuring Air, Water, Soil, and Workplace Safety Through Advanced Analytical Assessment

Environmental monitoring involves systematic testing of air, water, soil, emissions, and workplace conditions to assess environmental quality, public health risks, and regulatory compliance. Through detailed analysis of pollutants, contaminants, and physical or biological hazards, environmental monitoring provides essential data for environmental protection, industrial hygiene, and sustainable operations.

At METS Laboratories, we deliver a complete portfolio of environmental monitoring services using ISO/IEC 17025-accredited methodologies. Our testing supports regulatory compliance with global and regional standards, including EPA, ISO, OSHA, EN, BS, AS/NZS, and GCC environmental frameworks.












1. What Environmental Monitoring Covers

Environmental monitoring encompasses a wide range of matrices and workplace exposure factors, including:

Air Quality (Indoor, Ambient & Emissions)

  • Particulate matter (PM₂.₅, PM₁₀)
  • Nitrogen oxides (NO, NO₂, NOx)
  • Sulfur dioxide (SO₂)
  • Carbon monoxide (CO)
  • Ozone (O₃)
  • Volatile organic compounds (VOCs)
  • BTEX (Benzene, Toluene, Ethylbenzene, Xylene)
  • Heavy metals (Pb, As, Hg, Cr)

Water Quality

  • Drinking water
  • Process water
  • Wastewater & effluent
  • Groundwater & surface water
  • Irrigation & agricultural water

Soil and Surface Analysis

  • Contaminant quantification
  • Heavy metals
  • Microbial load
  • Organic and inorganic residue analysis

Occupational Hygiene & Workplace Exposure

  • Noise
  • Heat stress
  • Illumination (Lux)
  • Chemical exposure
  • Microbial monitoring
  • Indoor air quality

2. Environmental Monitoring Services Offered by METS

Below is the professionally structured list of METS environmental monitoring capabilities, aligned with international regulatory standards.

A. Ambient Air Quality Monitoring

We perform high-precision monitoring of ambient (outdoor) air pollutants using advanced real-time analyzers and reference laboratory techniques.

Pollutants Monitored

  • PM₁₀ / PM₂.₅
  • SO₂
  • NO, NO₂, NOx
  • CO
  • O₃
  • VOCs
  • BTX
  • Heavy Metals

Applicable Standards

  • AS/NZS 3580.12.1
  • AS 3580.5.1
  • AS 3580.4.1
  • AS 3580.7.1
  • AS 3580.6.1
  • BS EN 60079-29-2
  • EPA-454/R-99-005
  • ISO 21501-4
  • OSHA Technical Manual (OTM), Section III

These standards ensure accurate detection of pollutants and compliance with environmental regulatory frameworks.

B. Noise Monitoring (Environmental & Occupational)

Parameters Measured

  • Lₑq (Equivalent Continuous Noise Level)
  • Lₘₐₓ / Lₘᵢₙ
  • L_dn (Day-Night Noise Levels)
  • L_peak (Impulse Noise)

METS also provides Personnel Noise Exposure Monitoring using calibrated noise dosimeters to measure individual worker exposure.

Standards Followed

  • OSHA 29 CFR 1910.95
  • NIOSH RELs
  • ISO 9612
  • Local regulatory requirements

These assessments help determine worker safety compliance and noise mitigation requirements.

C. Stack Emission Monitoring

We conduct U.S. EPA-approved stack emission testing for industrial air pollutants using Direct Sensor Readout instrumentation (Lancom III).

Key Pollutants Measured

  • PM
  • VOCs
  • SOₓ
  • NOₓ
  • CO
  • Heavy metals
  • Moisture

Applicable Standards

  • USEPA CTM-030 / CTM-034
  • USEPA Method 10 (CO)
  • USEPA Method 6C (SO₂)
  • USEPA Method 1 & 2 (Velocity traverses)
  • USEPA Method 5 (PM)
  • USEPA Method 4 (Moisture)

These methods provide legally defensible and regulator-approved emission data.

D. Lux (Illuminance) Monitoring

We measure workplace lighting using fully calibrated digital lux meters to ensure compliance with occupational and visual comfort standards.

Standard Followed

  • BS 667:2005

Used for:

  • Lighting compliance audits
  • Worker ergonomics evaluations
  • Energy and illumination optimization

E. Heat Stress Monitoring

Our heat stress assessments follow the OSHA Technical Manual (2017) and ISO/ACGIH guidelines.

Parameters Evaluated

  • Dry Bulb Temperature (DBT)
  • Wet Bulb Temperature (WBT)
  • Globe Temperature
  • Relative Humidity
  • Air Velocity
  • Heat Index
  • WBGT (Wet Bulb Globe Temperature) — Indoor & outdoor metrics

This helps organizations maintain worker safety in extreme-temperature environments.

F. Dioxins & Furans Testing

We perform ultra-trace analysis of dioxins and furans in ambient air and stack emissions using:

Analytical Technique

  • HRGC–HRMS (High-Resolution Gas Chromatography – High-Resolution Mass Spectrometry)

Regulatory Standards

  • EU Directive 2010/75/EU
  • USEPA Method 23 / TO-9A
  • Stockholm Convention on POPs

Our testing provides pg/Nm³-level sensitivity required for regulatory compliance.

G. Environmental Microbial Monitoring (Air, Surface, Personnel)

Essential for pharmaceuticals, biotech, healthcare, cleanrooms, laboratories.

Air Monitoring

  • Passive settling plates
  • Active air sampling (precision microbial quantification)

Surface Monitoring

  • Contact plates
  • Swab tests

Personnel Hygiene Monitoring

  • Gloves
  • Garments
  • Fingertips

Benefits

  • Validates aseptic conditions
  • Ensures GMP/ISO compliance
  • Prevents contamination
  • Protects product quality and patient safety
