Chemical Testing

Comprehensive Chemical Testing Services for All Industries

Chemical Testing is one of the most essential scientific processes in modern industry. It ensures that raw materials, finished products, and environmental samples meet quality, safety, and regulatory standards.

At METS Lab, our ISO/IEC 17025-accredited chemical testing services support industries ranging from pharmaceuticals and food to construction, manufacturing, and water treatment.

Through advanced chemical analysis, we determine the composition, purity, and performance characteristics of materials according to global standards — including ASTM, ISO, BIS, AOAC, and EPA.



Why Chemical Testing Matters

Chemical testing helps industries guarantee that every material used or produced is safe, compliant, and high-performing.

It identifies and quantifies the chemical components in solids, liquids, gases, or mixtures using precise analytical methods.

Key Benefits of Chemical Testing

  • Verifies raw material purity and suitability for production.

  • Detects impurities, adulterants, or contaminants.

  • Ensures compliance with environmental, health, and safety regulations.

  • Confirms product certification and customer specifications.

  • Supports research, development, and quality control.

Chemical testing combines qualitative (what’s in it) and quantitative (how much is in it) analysis using methods such as titration, spectroscopy, chromatography, and elemental analysis.

Applications of Chemical Testing Across Industries

Chemical Testing supports a broad range of industrial and commercial sectors:

  • Pharmaceuticals: Raw material and API purity verification.

  • Food & Beverage: Additive safety and labeling compliance.

  • Water Treatment: Industrial, boiler, and potable water analysis.

  • Construction & Building Materials: Cement, lime, and admixture testing.

  • Petrochemicals & Lubricants: Viscosity, acid number, and contamination analysis.

  • Environmental Monitoring: Effluent discharge, soil, and air quality compliance.

  • Polymers & Plastics: Additive and heavy metal content evaluation.

  • Fertilizers: Nutrient balance and purity analysis.

Each chemical test is performed using validated, standard methods recognized by international regulatory authorities.
